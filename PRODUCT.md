# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Iranian small business owners and founders who currently rely on Instagram or have no web presence. They arrive skeptical — cost, bad past experiences, or not knowing they needed a site — but motivated by the fear of losing ground to competitors. They decide fast: the page either earns trust on first scroll or they leave. Context: mobile-first, Farsi-reading, often arriving from social or word of mouth. English-speaking visitors (international / diaspora clients) are a secondary audience served by the bilingual toggle.

## Product Purpose

Hasan Shahmoradi's personal services landing page. It does one thing: convert a skeptical visitor into a free-landing-page lead. The free offer eliminates price risk; the form qualifies intent and gathers brief details. Success looks like a completed form submission and a scheduled contact within 24 hours. Every section must advance that conversion — credibility → urgency → desire → action.

## Brand Personality

Confident, Direct, Premium. The closer who already knows the answer before the client asks. Not a vendor; an expert who is doing the prospect a favor by offering the free landing page. Warmth exists but is never soft — it's the warmth of someone who has done this many times and knows it will work.

## Anti-references

- Generic Persian web-dev portfolios: dark blue + teal + floating card templates. Forgettable.
- Upwork / Fiverr contractor aesthetics: commodity pricing signals, no premium positioning.
- SaaS-cream AI scaffold: warm neutral backgrounds, eyebrows on every section, gradient text, side-stripe cards. Looks generated, not crafted.
- Over-polished Western agency sites that feel foreign to the Iranian SMB market — the site should feel like it was made by someone who understands the Iranian digital landscape specifically.

## Design Principles

1. **The free offer is the product.** Every word and pixel serves the lead-generation goal. Nothing decorative that doesn't advance trust or desire.
2. **Premium without distance.** High craft signals competence; approachable copy signals a real person. The two must coexist — luxury feel, zero jargon, direct tone.
3. **Pain before promise.** The audience is skeptical. Name their specific fear (Instagram going down, competitors eating their traffic) before offering the solution.
4. **RTL-native, not RTL-adapted.** Persian is not a cosmetic switch. Typography, spacing, hierarchy, and copy rhythm are built for right-to-left reading from the ground up.
5. **Show the work.** Portfolio images and real client results carry more weight than any copy claim. The design must make space for evidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as minimum. RTL layout must be fully native (not CSS mirroring of LTR layout). Reduced-motion support required — the page relies heavily on GSAP animations and particle canvas; all must degrade gracefully. Color contrast: gold (#e5c100) on near-black is fine for large/bold text but must be checked for body-size usage. Bilingual mode (Farsi/English toggle) must be complete and tested — not just a language swap but a full directional and typographic switch.
